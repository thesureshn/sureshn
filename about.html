Research Areas

My research work spans multiple areas in Artificial Intelligence, with a primary focus on Video Quality Assessment and its applications in modern video processing systems. Below are the key areas of my research:

  • No-reference Video Quality Assessment (VQA) using Deep Learning: Developing advanced machine learning models to evaluate video quality without requiring a reference video, enabling real-time assessments for streaming platforms and other video processing applications.
  • Video Processing, Compression, and Streaming: Working on algorithms to optimize video encoding, compression, and streaming, improving efficiency and quality for real-world applications such as video conferencing and OTT platforms.
  • Computer Vision and Image Analysis: Enabling machines to interpret and analyze visual data for tasks like object detection, image classification, and scene understanding.
  • Deep Learning for Action Recognition and Temporal Features: Leveraging temporal dynamics in videos to identify actions, events, and patterns, which is critical for surveillance, sports analytics, and human activity recognition.
  • Optical Character Recognition (OCR) for Low-resource Languages: Building OCR systems tailored for languages with limited datasets, enabling digitization and accessibility for underrepresented linguistic communities.
  • Representation Learning and Transfer Learning: Creating generalized feature representations that can be transferred across tasks, reducing the need for extensive data and accelerating model training for novel problems.
